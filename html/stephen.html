STEPHEN KING

Stephen King, born in 1947 in Portland, Maine, is a renowned author known for his captivating horror and supernatural fiction. Raised by his mother after his parents' separation, King's childhood was spent in various locations, including Fort Wayne, Indiana, and Stratford, Connecticut.

He attended the University of Maine at Orono, where he wrote for the school newspaper and actively participated in student politics. Despite graduating with a degree in English and qualification to teach, health issues prevented him from pursuing a teaching career.

In 1971, he married Tabitha Spruce, and they began their life together while King worked odd jobs and continued to write. His breakthrough came with the publication of his novel "Carrie" in 1974, followed by numerous successful works such as "The Shining" and "The Stand."

King's success allowed him to focus on writing full-time, and he has since become one of the most prolific and successful authors of his generation. His contributions to literature have earned him numerous awards, including The National Book Foundation Medal for Distinguished Contribution to American Letters and the National Medal of Arts.

Beyond his literary achievements, King is also known for his philanthropy, providing scholarships for local students and supporting various charities. He continues to reside in Maine with his wife, Tabitha, and their three children.
